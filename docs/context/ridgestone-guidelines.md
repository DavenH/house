## Ridgestone Dream Home — Constraints & Desires 
I’m designing a “forever” home on wooded land in Keswick Ridge (New Brunswick, Canada), at the end of a ~200m driveway. The house will look out over a river valley / water view, with the best views generally to the **west**. Climate is cold/snowy; winter comfort and energy efficiency matter. 

### Build + Practical Constraints
 * Designed for **me now (single)**, but should comfortably support a **future 2-child family**.
 * **No basement**; I want a **slab-on-grade concrete pad** foundation.
 * I’ll do much of the construction myself; I have heavy equipment and can weld.
 * Budget roughly **$300k for materials + labour**, with substantial savings because I can mill a lot of my own timber.
 * Primary heat: **firewood**, with a strong preference for a **central masonry/hearth thermal mass** for efficiency; possibly add a geothermal heat pump later.
 * Mechanical/service spaces should be practical (laundry, utilities, storage) and not steal prime view/light areas. 

### Aesthetic Goals
 * Exterior vibe: **timber frame + natural stone masonry**, “rustic ski lodge” meets warm Frank Lloyd Wright-ish interior feeling.
 * I like **tasteful asymmetry** and a **mountainous roofline** (hip roofs / gables; “castle-ish” but not extravagant; avoid warehouse/commercial boxiness).
 * I want **lots of natural light**, but also **cozy winter comfort**.
 * A **tower element** exists in the massing; the **tower’s first level will be closet/storage**. 

### Orientation / Light Preferences
 * West light and west views are prized; kitchen and main living areas should leverage that.
 * I want the **kitchen to be bright** and ideally have **west-facing windows**.
 * The **grand piano must not sit in direct sunlight** (stable humidity/light), but I’d like it to be **visible from multiple viewpoints** in the main spaces. 

### Core Room Program — Main Level
 * **Kitchen**: bright, well lit, west views.
 * **Dining**: close to kitchen; good natural light.
 * **Pantry**: directly adjacent to kitchen; lighting not important.
 * **Great room / family room**: near the hearth; comfortable seating; part of the social heart.
 * **Ground-floor bathroom**: should be **grand / spa-like** (high-end inspiration), not a tiny powder room.
 * **Office**: on the main level.
 * **Entry + mudroom**: spacious enough for multiple people arriving with winter gear; wants good storage and flow.
 * **Laundry / machine room**: can be main or upper level; needs practical access and good stacking with plumbing.
 * **Storage / extra rooms**: a couple of flexible spaces for future kids, guests, studio overflow, or general storage. 

### Upper Level Program (high level)
 * **Master bedroom location updated**: it will be in the **north-facing gable** (large), because it has a special view (a big pine tree).
 * Master suite includes **walk-in closet + ensuite bathroom**; ensuite should still stack logically over/near the main-level bathroom for plumbing efficiency.
 * **Pool table lounge** likely upstairs (needs enough clearance for an 8'×4' table plus comfortable margins). 

### Key Planning Principles
 * **Central hearth/chimney mass** for heating efficiency and as the social/visual anchor.
 * Clear zoning:
 * “Service core” (bath/laundry/mechanical/storage) compact and efficient.
 * “View/light core” (kitchen/dining/great room) oriented to west light and scenery.
 * Quiet rooms (office/flex rooms) placed for calm and privacy.
 * Circulation should feel natural and not waste space; stair location needs to be planned (tower is storage, not stair).
